Local sellers bring resale clothing stores the inventory — pre‑screened against the
store's own buying rules — and the store picks what makes the rack.
**Decision‑support, not automation** — the AI recommends, the manager approves.

## Target customers
Consignment stores · thrift boutiques · children's resale · women's resale boutiques ·
outdoor gear resale · sneaker & streetwear resale · small multi‑location chains.

## Four value pillars (reuse across home, features, pricing)
1. **Inventory comes to you, pre‑filtered.** A curated feed of local items that already
   match your brands, categories, condition floor, and price band — instead of walk‑ins
   with bags of random clothes.
2. **No wasted counter time.** The AI rejects off‑brand, off‑category, and poor‑condition
   items before they ever reach your queue.
3. **You stay in control.** Approve or reject each item from your phone; a manager approves
   before anything moves; your buying rules, your data.
4. **Drop‑offs handle themselves.** Approve an item and the seller is automatically notified
   to schedule a drop‑off — no phone tag. POS‑ready CSV export when you want it.

## The setup offer (what your store gets)
1. Your branded seller link + QR ("scan to sell")
2. Store buying rule setup
3. Brand allowlist / blocklist
4. Pricing rubric
5. AI pre‑screening against your rules
6. Manager approval workflow
7. Automatic seller drop‑off invites
8. POS‑ready title + description and CSV export
9. Weekly inventory + pricing reporting

## How it works (5 steps)
1. **Share your link** — drop your seller link / QR on Instagram, your window, your receipts.
2. **Sellers submit** — locals photograph front, back, and tag of items for your store.
3. **AI pre‑screens** — every item is checked against your buying rules; only matches reach you.
4. **You curate** — approve or pass on each piece from your phone; a manager confirms.
5. **Seller drops off** — approved sellers are notified to book a drop‑off time.

## Trust pillar (front and center)
"Tag to Rack prepares recommendations. It never finalizes a purchase, price, or listing
on its own — a store manager approves every decision." Confidence scores, editable fields,
full audit trail.
