# Tag to Rack — Pricing analysis for small local consignment shops
*June 2026 · prepared for Conner*

## What the market actually pays (software)

Small consignment shops already pay for ONE core system (their POS/consignment tracker):

| Product | Monthly price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| ConsignCloud | $49 / $99 / $199 / $299 | Most popular entry point; integrations included |
| SimpleConsign | ~$129–$359 | Three tiers; add-ons cost extra |
| Ricochet | $199/location | +$49–79 ecommerce, +$100 if not using their payments |
| Aravenda | $289 / $449 / $559 | Premium end; includes Shopify site |

**The realistic budget window for this segment is roughly $100–$300/month**, with $300–$550 only for shops that are bigger or buying an all-in-one.

## What the shops themselves can afford

- Small clothing/consignment stores commonly do **$50k–$150k/year revenue**; established ones in good locations reach $180k–$1M.
- Net margins run **4–13%**; the average consignment-store owner takes home ≈ **$52k/year**.
- At $1,500/month ($18k/yr), the current Sourcing OS price equals **12–36% of a small shop's total revenue** — more than most owners' rent and far above any software line item they've ever signed. For this segment it reads as a typo, not a price.
- $1,500/mo is only rational for high-volume stores ($500k+) where it visibly replaces a part-time hire (~$1,200–$1,800/mo at 20 hrs/wk).

## The honest positioning question

Tag to Rack is a **sourcing channel**, not a POS — it's additive revenue, which justifies pricing above the $49 floor. But it's also *unproven* (no case studies yet), and unproven tools price at the bottom of their value band, then climb with evidence.

## Recommendation

**Trial: keep $499 for two months.** It's well-calibrated — high enough to filter tire-kickers and cover your hands-on setup, low enough to be a no-brainer against a $1,200/mo part-timer. Frame it as a *founding-partner pilot*, credited toward the first month (as the site already says).

**Monthly: drop the list price into the believable band, with tiers:**

| Tier | Price | For |
|---|---|---|
| **Starter** | **$149/mo** — up to ~75 AI-screened submissions | shops under ~$150k/yr |
| **Standard** | **$299/mo** — ~250 submissions, market-comp pricing, digest notifications, weekly reports | the sweet spot; most pilots convert here |
| **Multi-location / concierge** | **$599+/mo** | chains, or "we run it for you" |

Why this isn't "being dumb cheap":
1. **$299 is ~2–3× ConsignCloud/SimpleConsign** — premium, justified by "brings inventory in" vs "tracks inventory."
2. **Your unit costs are tiny** (Gemini per-item pennies, Cloudflare ~free) — 90%+ gross margin even at $149.
3. **Patience play:** give your first 3–5 pilot stores a *founding rate* — $199/mo locked for 12 months in exchange for a case study + referrals. Collect hard ROI numbers (items sourced, revenue added, hours saved). Once you can show "Store X added $2,400/mo of rack inventory," you raise the list price with proof instead of hope — that's when $499–$999/mo becomes sellable to the bigger shops.
4. **Anchor pricing to value visibly:** keep the "less than a part-time hire" framing, and when the eBay-comps engine is live, show each store its monthly "value sourced" number in the weekly report — that's your future price-increase ammunition.

**Net:** $499 trial → $299/mo Standard (with $149 Starter and a $199 founding rate for the first cohort). Expect $3.6k ARR/store initially; at 10 stores that's $36k ARR with near-zero marginal cost, and a credible path to 2–3× pricing once case studies exist.

*Sources: ConsignCloud, SimpleConsign, Ricochet and Aravenda published/reviewed pricing (2026); small-clothing-store revenue and margin benchmarks. Not financial advice — final pricing is a business judgment call.*
