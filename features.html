Features

Everything the setup includes.

Tag to Rack is a focused toolkit for sourcing from local sellers — from your "scan to sell" link to a curated, pre-screened queue, with a manager approval step holding it all together.

01

Your seller link & QR

A branded "scan to sell" link and QR code for your store. Sellers run a guided front / back / tag capture from their own phone — consistent every time.

02

AI pre-screening

Every submission is checked against your rules, with brand, category, size, color, material, condition, and possible flaws drafted automatically — each with a confidence indicator. Junk is filtered out before it reaches you.

03

Your selection criteria

We configure the criteria you select by — accepted brands, quality and condition standards, seasonality, categories, and price bands — so every submission is scored against what your store actually wants.

04

Brand allowlist & blocklist

The brands you want and the brands to auto-reject, loaded in — so the AI surfaces the names that matter and screens out the ones that don't.

05

Pricing rubric

Suggested price ranges grounded in your own rubric and buy / pass / consign logic — never a black-box number.

06

Automatic drop-off invites

Approve an item and the seller is automatically emailed to book a drop-off time — no phone tag, no scheduling back-and-forth.

07

POS-ready listings & export

Approved items become clean, consistent titles and descriptions and export as a tidy, mapped CSV that drops straight into your point of sale.

08

Weekly inventory & pricing reporting

A simple weekly view of what came in, how it was priced, and where the queue stands — built for managers.

09

Management approval workflow

The backbone of the system: a review queue where a manager approves every buy, price, and listing, with a full audit trail.

Trust by design

Decision-support, never autopilot.

Tag to Rack is built to make a good buyer faster — not to replace their judgment. The control stays with your team.

  • Every recommendation is editable before approval.
  • Confidence scores show where to look twice.
  • A manager signs off before anything is bought, priced, or listed.
  • Full audit trail of who approved what, and when.
